Introduction¶

In this project I am trying to look at the characteristics of money making movies from the perspective of an investor in a movie. The main question that I am trying to answer with this project is

Which actors, directors and genres should an investor look at when he wants to invest his money in a new movie project ?

The data used in this project is a cleaned version of the IMDB database.

In this project a profitable project is a movie that succeeds to cover it's costs - that is a movie where the difference between the budget and the revenue is positive (it makes a profit).

In order to find these kind of movies we will be looking at who tends to star in these movies, who tends to direct them and what genre they tend to be.

It might sound like logical acrobatics, but the approach that I am using to determine the answers in the title is as follows:
I will compute the average profit of the movies in which an actor plays, this way if actor Syl played in 10 movies and all these movies had a profit 100 USD it means that the average profits of the movies in which Syl played is 100. I will also do this for the Directors and Genres categories This is not a perfect approach, in fact, let's be honest, it has very many flows, what does it measure is if the actor tends to play, on average in profitable movies or in less profitable ones.
as I said earlier, Iwill proceed in similar fashion for directors and genres

Conclusions¶

The relevant conclusions were drawn at the end of each section. As a conclusion for this paper, I want to emphasize again the limitations and the approach taken to arrive at the above mentioned conclusions.
First the approach
I took the actors, namely one actor, and divided the sum of the profits of every movie he has played in by the said number of movies. This number tells us the average profit of the movies in which said actor was cast. This way a potential investor can have a reliable picture of the averages of the factors analysed above. Yet, art does not abide by the law of the averages we saw that the "profit" for one actor is two deciles further apart to the next one, we also saw the directors and genres that tend to be succesfull, again on average.
Now, the limitations of this approach is that we cannot use this data to reliably asses if the next movie the said actor will play in will be a profitable movie or it is going to be a flop. It tells the investor where he could look
